Common Printer Problems: Causes, Fixes & Troubleshooting Guide

Printer & Services Team July 9, 2026 8 min read

Printer problems are frustrating, especially when you need to print something urgently. Whether you own an HP, EPSON, BROTHER, or CANON printer, most common printer issues share similar causes and solutions. In this complete troubleshooting guide, we will walk you through the most frequent printer problems and show you how to fix them quickly.

Quick Fact: Over 80% of common printer problems can be resolved remotely without any hardware replacement. Many issues are caused by software, drivers, or network connectivity rather than actual hardware failure.

1. Printer Offline Error (Most Common Printer Problem)

The printer offline error is the #1 most common issue reported by printer users. Your printer shows offline in Windows even though it is powered on. Here are the main causes:

  • WiFi disconnection: Printer lost connection to your wireless network after a router restart or internet outage.
  • Outdated or corrupt printer drivers: Old drivers cause communication failures between your computer and printer.
  • USB cable issues: Loose or faulty USB cables make the printer appear offline.
  • Windows print spooler errors: A stuck print job in the queue can cause the printer to show offline.
  • Power saving mode: The printer enters sleep mode and does not wake up properly.

Quick Fix: Restart both your printer and computer. On Windows 11, go to Settings > Bluetooth & Devices > Printers & Scanners, select your printer, and uncheck "Use Printer Offline" if it is enabled. Run the HP Print and Scan Doctor tool for automatic diagnosis if you have an HP printer.

2. Printer Not Connecting to WiFi

Wireless connectivity issues are extremely common, especially after changing internet providers or router settings. If your printer is not connecting to WiFi, check these common causes:

  • Router distance: The printer is too far from the router, causing a weak WiFi signal.
  • Network band mismatch: Most printers only support 2.4GHz networks, but your router may be broadcasting on 5GHz.
  • Incorrect WiFi password: WiFi passwords are case-sensitive. Double-check for typos.
  • Network congestion: Too many devices connected to the same network can interfere.
  • Router firewall settings: Security settings may be blocking the printer.

Quick Fix: Move the printer within 10-15 feet of the router. Switch your router to broadcast on 2.4GHz band. Use the WPS button method for the easiest wireless setup. Most HP printers also support the HP Smart app for guided WiFi setup.

3. Poor Print Quality (Faded Prints, Streaks, Lines)

If your prints are coming out faded, with streaks, lines, or blurry output, you are dealing with print quality problems. This is a common printer issue that affects all brands including HP, EPSON, BROTHER, and CANON.

  • Clogged printhead: Ink dries up when the printer is not used for a long time, clogging the printhead nozzles.
  • Low ink or toner: One or more cartridges may be empty or running low.
  • Incorrect paper type setting: Using photo paper settings on plain paper causes ink smudging.
  • Low-quality or third-party ink: Refilled or generic cartridges often produce poor print quality.
  • Dirty printhead or rollers: Dust and ink residue accumulate over time.

Quick Fix: Run a printhead cleaning cycle from your printer's maintenance menu. Use genuine manufacturer cartridges. Select the correct paper type in your print settings before printing. For HP printers, run the HP Print and Scan Doctor tool to diagnose print quality issues.

4. Paper Jams

Paper jams are annoying but usually easy to fix. Common causes of printer paper jams include:

  • Overfilled paper tray: Too much paper prevents proper feeding.
  • Worn out rollers: After extensive use, rollers lose grip and cannot pull paper through.
  • Wrong paper type or size: Using paper that your printer does not support.
  • Curled or damp paper: Old or moisture-affected paper curls and causes jams.
  • Paper debris inside: Small pieces of torn paper stuck inside the printer.

Quick Fix: Do not overfill the paper tray — keep it at 75% capacity. Always use the paper type recommended in your printer manual. Clean the rollers gently with a soft, lint-free cloth slightly dampened with water. Remove any paper debris carefully.

5. Printer Driver Problems

Printer driver issues are among the most common printer problems, especially after Windows updates. The driver is the software that allows your computer to communicate with the printer.

  • Corrupt driver: Windows updates or malware can corrupt printer drivers.
  • Outdated driver: Old drivers are incompatible with new Windows 11 versions.
  • Wrong driver: You installed a driver for a different printer model.
  • Driver conflict: Multiple printer drivers installed simultaneously cause conflicts.

Quick Fix: Always download the latest driver from the official manufacturer website. Uninstall the old driver completely before installing a new one. For HP printers, the HP Smart app automatically detects and installs the correct driver. You can also use the HP Print and Scan Doctor tool for automatic driver diagnosis and repair.

6. Printer in Error State (Windows)

Seeing "Printer in error state" in Windows? This is a common printer issue that prevents any print jobs from going through. The error state usually means Windows cannot communicate with the printer properly.

  • Print spooler service stopped: The Windows print spooler may have crashed.
  • Stuck print jobs: A failed print job is blocking the queue.
  • Connection issues: USB or network connection problems trigger the error state.
  • Corrupt printer settings: Damaged printer configuration in Windows.

Quick Fix: Restart the Print Spooler service (services.msc > Print Spooler > Restart). Clear all print jobs from the queue. Remove the printer from Devices & Printers and re-add it. Run your printer manufacturer's diagnostic tool.

7. How to Prevent Printer Problems

Prevention is always better than cure. Follow these tips to avoid common printer problems:

  • Use your printer regularly: Print at least 2-3 times per week to prevent ink from drying.
  • Keep drivers updated: Install the latest drivers from the manufacturer's official website.
  • Use genuine ink and toner: Original cartridges provide better print quality and fewer issues.
  • Clean your printer monthly: Remove dust and debris from inside the printer.
  • Use quality paper: Cheap paper causes more jams and print quality problems.
  • Use a surge protector: Protect your printer from power fluctuations and surges.
  • Always shut down properly: Do not turn off the printer directly from the power switch — use the power button.
