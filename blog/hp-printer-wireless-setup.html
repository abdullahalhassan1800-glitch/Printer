How to Connect HP Printer to WiFi

Three easy methods for wireless HP printer setup — WPS, HP Smart app, or manual configuration

Published: July 8, 2026 | Category: HP Printer Setup

Can't connect your HP printer to WiFi? You are not alone. HP printer wireless setup is one of the most common challenges HP printer owners face. Whether you need to configure a new WiFi printer setup or fix existing printer WiFi connection problems, this guide covers everything. Many printer connectivity issues are caused by simple network configuration problems that you can fix yourself.

Method 1: Connect Using WPS Button (Easiest)

If your router has a WPS button, this is the quickest method. Press the WPS button on your router for 3 seconds until the light starts blinking. Then, within 2 minutes, press the Wireless button on your HP printer. The printer will automatically detect and connect to your network. The entire process takes under 30 seconds.

Important:

Not all routers support WPS. Check your router manual or look for a physical WPS button. If your router does not have WPS, use Method 2 or 3 below.

Method 2: Connect Using HP Smart App

Download the HP Smart app from the Microsoft Store, Apple App Store, or Google Play Store. Open the app and create or sign in to your HP account. Click "Set Up a New Printer" and select your HP model. The app will guide you through selecting your WiFi network and entering your password.

Method 3: Manual Setup Using Wireless Setup Wizard

On your HP printer's touchscreen, navigate to Settings (or Setup) > Network > Wireless Setup Wizard. The printer will scan for available WiFi networks. Select your network from the list, enter the WiFi password using the on-screen keyboard, and confirm. The printer will connect to the network and print a confirmation page.

HP Printer WiFi Troubleshooting

  • Printer not finding network: Make sure your printer is within range of the router. Move it closer if needed.
  • Wrong password: WiFi passwords are case-sensitive. Double-check for typos.
  • Dual-band networks: HP printers typically work best on 2.4 GHz networks. If your router uses both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands, try connecting to the 2.4 GHz network.
  • Router restart: Restart your router by unplugging it for 30 seconds, then try the setup again.
  • Outdated printer drivers: Make sure you have the latest printer driver installation by checking HP's support website. Old drivers can prevent successful WiFi connection.

Optimize Your Wireless Printing Experience

After successfully completing your WiFi printer setup, use these HP features for a better experience:

  • HP Smart app: Manage your printer, check ink levels, and monitor network status from your phone.
  • Optimize and Print: This feature automatically adjusts wireless settings for the best print performance.
  • Print Anywhere: Print from any device, anywhere, using HP's cloud printing service. Perfect for when you need to print but are not near your printer.
  • Network security: Use WPA2 or WPA3 encryption on your router to keep your printer connection secure.
