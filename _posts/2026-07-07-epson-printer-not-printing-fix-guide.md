Is your printer not printing at all, or printing blank pages? Tank and business models — are prone to specific issues that cause this. Here are the most effective fixes.

### Why Printers Stop Printing

- **Clogged print head nozzles:** The #1 cause. Print heads clog when the printer sits unused for days.
- **Ink system issues:** Tank models can develop air bubbles in the ink lines after refilling.
- **Driver conflicts:** Windows updates sometimes break printer driver communication.
- **Incorrect default printer:** Your computer may be sending jobs to a different printer.
- **Waste ink pad saturation:** Printers have a built-in waste ink counter that stops printing when full.

### Fix 1: Run a Power Cleaning Cycle

Go to **Settings > Maintenance > Power Cleaning** on your printer. This uses more ink than a standard head cleaning but clears stubborn clogs. Run it 1-2 times, then print a nozzle check pattern.

### Fix 2: Check for Blank Ink Cartridges

Remove each cartridge and verify ink is actually flowing. For tank models, hold the cartridge up to light — you should see ink inside. If the tank looks empty, refill and prime the ink lines by running 2-3 cleaning cycles.

### Fix 3: Reinstall Printer Drivers

Uninstall the current printer driver from **Control Panel > Programs**. Download the latest driver from the manufacturer's support page for your exact model number. Install it and restart your computer.

### Fix 4: Reset the Waste Ink Pad Counter

If your printer shows "Service Required" or stops printing entirely, the waste ink pad counter may be full. Download the **WIC Reset Utility** to check and reset the counter. You may also need to replace the physical waste ink pads.

### Fix 5: Check Print Queue and Default Printer

Open **Settings > Printers & Scanners** on Windows. Make sure your printer is set as default and clear any stuck print jobs from the queue.
