Printer showing offline? Your printer appears offline in Windows when it cannot communicate properly with your computer. This is one of the most common printer issues and often causes the **printer not printing** problem as well. In most cases, the problem is software-related and can be fixed in minutes without a technician. Whether your **printer shows offline in Windows 11** or Windows 10, the solutions below will help.

### Why Does My Printer Show Offline?

The offline status usually means Windows detects the printer but cannot communicate with it. Common causes include loose USB connections, WiFi network changes, outdated drivers, or Windows print spooler issues. You may also see the **printer in error state** message, which indicates a similar communication problem. This is a classic **printer connectivity issue** that requires systematic **printer troubleshooting**.

### Step 1: Restart Your Printer and Computer
Turn off your printer and unplug the power cord. Wait 60 seconds, then plug it back in and turn it on. Restart your computer as well. This clears temporary errors and often resolves the offline status immediately.

### Step 2: Check Your Printer's Connection
If using a USB cable, try a different USB port. If using WiFi, print a network configuration page from your printer's menu to verify it is connected to the right network. Make sure both devices are on the same WiFi network.

### Step 3: Set Printer as Default
Open Windows Settings (Windows key + I) and go to Bluetooth & Devices > Printers & Scanners. Find your printer, click on it, and select "Set as default." This tells Windows to use this printer for all print jobs.

### Step 4: Disable "Use Printer Offline" Mode
Still in Printers & Scanners, click your printer and select "Open print queue." Go to the Printer menu in the top-left corner. If "Use Printer Offline" has a checkmark next to it, click it to uncheck the option.

### Step 5: Run Diagnostic Tool
Download your printer manufacturer's diagnostic tool from the official support website. Run the tool and select your printer. It will automatically diagnose connectivity issues, driver problems, and spooler errors, then apply fixes.

### Additional Tips to Keep Your Printer Online

- **Keep Windows updated:** The latest Windows updates include improved printer drivers and fixes for known printer connectivity issues.
- **Use your printer's companion app:** Your printer's companion app helps you monitor printer status, check ink levels, and resolve problems before they cause the printer to go offline.
- **Enable Optimize and Print:** Your printer's auto-optimization feature adjusts print settings automatically to prevent spooler conflicts and reduce offline errors.
- **Use Print Anywhere:** With your printer's cloud printing feature, you can print from anywhere without worrying about local network connectivity issues.
- **Check for firmware updates:** Outdated printer firmware can cause the printer to disconnect from WiFi networks randomly. Check for updates in your printer's companion app or printer menu.

### What If These Steps Don't Work?

If your printer is still showing offline after trying all the steps above, the issue may be more complex. Common causes include corrupt Windows system files, advanced network configuration problems, or hardware-level communication failures. Consider contacting the manufacturer's official support for model-specific assistance.
