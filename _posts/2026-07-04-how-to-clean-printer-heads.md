Clogged print heads cause faded prints, missing colors, streaks, and blank pages. Regardless of printer type, here is how to clean the print heads properly.

### Automatic Cleaning (Try This First)

Every printer has a built-in cleaning cycle accessible from the printer menu or your computer's printer settings.

**On Windows:**
1. Open **Settings > Printers & Scanners**
2. Select your printer > **Printer Properties**
3. Click the **Maintenance** tab
4. Select **Nozzle Check** first, then **Head Cleaning** if needed

**On Mac:**
1. Open **System Preferences > Printers & Scanners**
2. Select your printer > **Options & Supplies**
3. Click **Utility** tab > **Open Printer Utility**
4. Run **Nozzle Check** and **Head Cleaning**

Run 2-3 cleaning cycles, printing a nozzle check pattern after each. If patterns improve, the clog is clearing. If not after 3 cycles, try manual cleaning.

### Manual Cleaning (For Stubborn Clogs)

**What you need:** Distilled water, lint-free cloths, rubber gloves, isopropyl alcohol (for dried ink)

**Step-by-step:**
1. Turn off and unplug the printer
2. Open the printer and locate the print head (usually in the carriage assembly)
3. Remove ink cartridges carefully
4. Remove the print head if it's detachable (check your model's manual)
5. Soak the print head nozzle plate in distilled water for 10-15 minutes
6. For dried ink, use a cloth dampened with isopropyl alcohol
7. Gently wipe the electrical contacts — do not touch the nozzle plate directly
8. Let the print head dry completely (at least 15 minutes)
9. Reinstall everything and run 2 cleaning cycles

### Prevention Tips

- Print at least once a week to keep ink flowing
- Use genuine cartridges — third-party ink clogs more often
- Turn off the printer properly instead of using the power strip
- Keep the printer in a dust-free, temperature-stable environment
